topic: Wheat Cultivation Guide
crop: Wheat

## Overview
Wheat (Triticum aestivum) is the world's most widely cultivated cereal crop and a primary source of carbohydrates and protein. It is a cool-season crop suited to temperate and subtropical climates.

## Ideal Growing Conditions
- Temperature: 10–25 °C during vegetative growth; 15–20 °C during grain filling. Frost tolerance during early growth, but sensitive at flowering.
- Rainfall: 450–650 mm well distributed; irrigation critical in drier regions during jointing and grain fill.
- Humidity: 50–65%; low humidity during ripening improves grain quality and reduces disease.
- Sunlight: 7–9 hours per day; long days promote heading and maturity.
- Altitude: Widely grown from 0 to 3000 m; cool highlands are ideal.

## Soil Requirements
- Soil type: Well-drained loam or clay-loam soils; poor drainage causes root rot.
- pH: 6.0–7.5; optimal 6.5–7.0.
- Nitrogen: 100–120 kg N/ha; split doses at sowing, tillering, and booting stages.
- Phosphorus: 50–60 kg P₂O₅/ha as basal application; promotes root development.
- Potassium: 40–60 kg K₂O/ha; improves straw strength and disease tolerance.

## Planting Season
- Rabi (winter) crop in South Asia: October–November sowing, March–April harvest.
- Temperate regions: Autumn sowing (winter wheat) or spring sowing (spring wheat).
- Crop duration: 100–150 days depending on variety and location.

## Crop Management
- Seed rate: 100–125 kg/ha for irrigated; 125–150 kg/ha for rainfed conditions.
- Seed treatment with carbendazim (2 g/kg) or thiram to prevent seed-borne diseases.
- First irrigation at crown root initiation stage (21 DAS) is critical.
- 4–6 irrigations needed under irrigated conditions; avoid waterlogging.

## Common Pests
- Aphids: Suck sap from leaves and ears; use imidacloprid or dimethoate spray.
- Termites: Damage roots and stems; apply chlorpyrifos to soil at sowing.
- Wheat stem sawfly: Causes stem cutting near soil; rotate with non-host crops.

## Common Diseases
- Yellow rust (Puccinia striiformis): Yellow stripes on leaves; use resistant varieties, apply propiconazole.
- Brown rust (Puccinia triticina): Orange-brown pustules; apply mancozeb or tebuconazole.
- Loose smut (Ustilago tritici): Smutted ears instead of grains; use systemic seed treatment (carboxin).
- Karnal bunt: Partial bunt of grains; use clean certified seed, apply tebuconazole.

## Best Practices
- Use certified high-yielding varieties (e.g., semi-dwarf varieties with lodging resistance).
- Balanced fertilization with zinc sulfate (25 kg/ha) on zinc-deficient soils.
- Weed management: Apply isoproturon + metsulfuron pre-emergence/early post-emergence.
- Harvest at full maturity (grain moisture ~12–14%) to avoid shattering losses.
- Proper post-harvest storage to prevent mycotoxin contamination.

## Expected Yield
- Rainfed: 2–3 tons/ha
- Irrigated HYV: 4–6 tons/ha
- High-input irrigated: 6–8 tons/ha
