topic: Maize Cultivation Guide
crop: Maize

## Overview
Maize (Zea mays), also called corn, is the third most important cereal crop worldwide after wheat and rice. It serves as food, animal feed, and industrial raw material. Maize is a warm-season crop with a high yield potential.

## Ideal Growing Conditions
- Temperature: 18–32 °C; optimal 21–27 °C. Frost-sensitive at all stages; temperatures above 35 °C during pollination cause poor kernel set.
- Rainfall: 500–800 mm per growing season; critical moisture needed during silking and grain fill.
- Humidity: 50–70%; high humidity combined with warmth promotes fungal diseases.
- Sunlight: 8–10 hours of full sunlight per day; maize is a C4 plant with high light-use efficiency.
- Altitude: Sea level to 2500 m; highland varieties adapted to cooler temperatures.

## Soil Requirements
- Soil type: Well-drained deep loam or sandy-loam soils; waterlogging even for short periods causes severe yield loss.
- pH: 5.8–7.0; optimal 6.0–6.8.
- Nitrogen: High feeder — 120–150 kg N/ha; apply in 3 splits (basal, knee-high, tasseling).
- Phosphorus: 60–80 kg P₂O₅/ha as basal; critical for early root development.
- Potassium: 40–60 kg K₂O/ha; improves stalk strength and drought tolerance.

## Planting Season
- Tropical regions: Year-round with irrigation; main season aligned with monsoon onset.
- Temperate regions: Spring planting after last frost; harvest in autumn.
- Crop duration: 90–120 days for most hybrid varieties.

## Crop Management
- Seed rate: 18–22 kg/ha; plant spacing 60×20 cm (75,000–80,000 plants/ha).
- Hybrid seeds recommended over open-pollinated varieties for yield advantage.
- Hill-up soil around stems at knee-high stage for lodging prevention.
- Detasseling required in seed production plots.

## Common Pests
- Fall armyworm (Spodoptera frugiperda): Invasive pest; feeds on whorls; apply emamectin benzoate or chlorantraniliprole early.
- Corn borer (Ostrinia nubilalis): Bores into stalk and ear; use Bt-based biopesticides.
- Maize weevil (Sitophilus zeamais): Post-harvest storage pest; treat grain with phosphine fumigation.

## Common Diseases
- Maize streak virus: Transmitted by leafhoppers; yellow streaks on leaves; use resistant varieties.
- Gray leaf spot (Cercospora zeae-maydis): Rectangular gray lesions; use fungicide (azoxystrobin) and resistant hybrids.
- Northern corn leaf blight: Long cigar-shaped lesions; apply mancozeb, use resistant varieties.
- Stalk rot (Fusarium/Pythium): Premature death; avoid stress, ensure good drainage.

## Best Practices
- Use certified hybrid seeds for 20–30% higher yield.
- Integrated soil fertility management: combine 5 t/ha FYM with chemical fertilizers.
- Intercrop with legumes (soybean, cowpea) to improve soil nitrogen and farmer income.
- Weed management: atrazine pre-emergence + hand weeding at 30 DAS.
- Scout regularly for fall armyworm from 7 DAS — early detection is critical.

## Expected Yield
- Local varieties: 2–3 tons/ha
- Improved OPVs: 3–5 tons/ha
- Hybrids under good management: 6–10 tons/ha
