topic: Peanut (Groundnut) Cultivation Guide
crop: Peanut

## Overview
Peanut, also called groundnut (Arachis hypogaea), is a warm-season legume grown primarily for edible oil, protein-rich food, and animal feed. Like soybean, it fixes atmospheric nitrogen. It is a geocarpic plant — pods develop underground after aerial flowering.

## Ideal Growing Conditions
- Temperature: 25–35 °C; optimal 28–30 °C. Frost-sensitive. Soil temperature at planting should be at least 18 °C for good germination.
- Rainfall: 500–700 mm during growing season; good moisture during pegging and pod fill critical; dry conditions at harvest preferred.
- Humidity: 50–70%; high humidity promotes leaf diseases and aflatoxin risk.
- Sunlight: 8–10 hours of bright sunshine; full sun essential for pod development.
- Altitude: 0–1500 m; best in warm tropical and subtropical lowlands.

## Soil Requirements
- Soil type: Light, well-drained sandy loam or loamy sand; loose soil essential for pegs to penetrate and pods to develop properly. Heavy clay soils restrict pod development.
- pH: 5.8–7.0; optimal 6.0–6.5.
- Calcium: Critical for pod fill — apply gypsum (500 kg/ha) broadcast at pegging stage; Ca deficiency causes "pops" (empty pods).
- Nitrogen: Minimal external N needed if inoculated with Bradyrhizobium; 15–20 kg N/ha as starter dose.
- Phosphorus: 40–60 kg P₂O₅/ha; promotes rooting and pod development.
- Potassium: 40–60 kg K₂O/ha.

## Planting Season
- India/West Africa: June–July (Kharif); harvest October–November.
- USA: April–May; harvest August–October.
- Crop duration: 90–130 days depending on type (runner, virginia, spanish, valencia).

## Crop Management
- Seed rate: 80–120 kg/ha; plant shelled seeds or whole pods at 30×10 cm spacing.
- Inoculate with Bradyrhizobium for nitrogen fixation.
- Earthing up/hilling: At 30 and 45 days after planting to facilitate peg penetration into soil.
- Gypsum application at pegging (40–45 DAS) is critical for pod fill and preventing bud necrosis.
- Avoid irrigation during last 2–3 weeks before harvest to allow pod maturity and ease of harvesting.

## Common Pests
- Thrips (Frankliniella schultzei): Vector of Groundnut bud necrosis disease (GBNV/TSWV); use imidacloprid seed treatment; control at early stages.
- Leaf miner (Aproaerema modicella): Mines into leaflets causing drying; use quinalphos or dimethoate.
- White grubs (Holotrichia spp.): Soil pest feeding on roots; apply chlorpyrifos at sowing.
- Pod borer: Bores into maturing pods; monitor and apply insecticide.
- Red hairy caterpillar: Mass defoliation; mechanical collection in early stages; use chlorpyrifos.

## Common Diseases
- Early leaf spot (Cercospora arachidicola): Circular brown spots; apply chlorothalonil or mancozeb at 15-day intervals from 30 DAS.
- Late leaf spot (Cercosporidium personatum): Darker, circular lesions; same management as early leaf spot.
- Groundnut rosette virus: Transmitted by aphids; severe mosaic and stunting; use resistant varieties, control aphids.
- Collar rot (Aspergillus niger): Blackening at collar region; treat seed with thiram + carbendazim.
- Aflatoxin (Aspergillus flavus): Toxin contamination especially under drought stress at pod fill; use drought-tolerant varieties; harvest on time; dry properly to <9% moisture.

## Aflatoxin Management — Critical
- Drought stress during pod fill dramatically increases aflatoxin risk.
- Irrigate during late pod fill if rainfall is insufficient.
- Harvest promptly at maturity — delays increase contamination.
- Dry pods to <9% moisture within 48 hours of harvest.
- Store in cool, dry, ventilated conditions.

## Best Practices
- Use certified disease-free seed; treat with fungicide+insecticide combination.
- Gypsum application is non-negotiable — commonly neglected and causes significant yield loss.
- Practice 2–3 year crop rotation with cereals; avoid following soybean (shared diseases).
- Monitor for thrips from emergence — GBNV/TSWV infection in first 3 weeks causes most yield loss.
- Do not peel or crack pods for testing at harvest — use the floatation test or color charts.

## Expected Yield
- Traditional/rainfed without inputs: 800–1200 kg/ha
- Improved varieties with good management: 1800–2500 kg/ha
- High-yield irrigated: 3000–4000 kg/ha
- Oil content: 44–56%; protein content: 22–30%
